Malai Kofta

Malai Kofta is made with paneer and is an absolute lovely curry. Serve it on a special ocaasion or make it for your everyday meal.

Ingredients

    For Kofta:
  1. Paneer - 300 g
  2. Boiled potato - 1 medium size
  3. Ginger - 1/2 tsp grated
  4. Green chili - 1 tsp finely chopped
  5. Coriander - 1 tbsp finely chopped
  6. Salt to taste
  7. Garam masala powder - 1/2 tsp
  8. Cornflour - 2 tbsp
  9. Cashewnuts - 1 tbsp finely chopped
  10. Grated dry coconut - 1 tbsp
  11. Raisins - 1 tbsp chopped
  12. Oil for frying
    13. For curry:
  13. Butter - 2 tbsp
  14. Oil - 2 tbsp
  15. Cloves - 3-4
  16. Black peppercorns - 4-5
  17. Black cardamom - 2
  18. Cinnamon - 1 inch stick
  19. Ginger garlic paste - 2 tsp
  20. Tomato puree - 4 tbsp
  21. Chili powder - 1/2 tsp
  22. Cashew nut paste - 2 tbsp soak a few cashew nuts in water for 15 minutes and grind in a blender to make a paste
  23. Milk - 1 cup
  24. Fresh cream - 3 tbsp
  25. Honey - 1 tbsp
  26. Salt to taste
  27. Lemon juice - 2 tsp
  28. Kasuri methi - 2 tsp

Method

    For Kofta:
  1. Mix cashew nuts, grated coconut and raisins in a bowl. Keep aside.
  2. Mix all the remaining ingredients in another bowl.
  3. Take small amount of the paneer mixture and make a little space in the center.
  4. Fill some coconut mixture in the center and bring back the ends together and make a round kofta. Make all the koftas in the same manner.
  5. Heat oil in a non stick pan and fry the koftas on medium heat till golden brown. Remove the koftas on a tissue lined plate. Keep aside.
    6. For Gravy:
  6. Heat butter and oil in a pan.
  7. First, add cloves, black peppercorns, black cardamom and cinnamon and fry for 20 seconds.
  8. Then, add ginger garlic paste and fry for a minute.
  9. Later, add tomato puree and fry for another minute.
  10. Next, add cashew nut paste, chili powder and milk and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  11. Lastly, add fresh cream, honey, salt to taste, lemon juice and kasuri methi and cook for a minute.
  12. Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding little water and cook till the mixture comes to a boil.
  13. Add the koftas in the gravy and give it a boil.
  14. Garnish with fresh coriander.
  15. Serve hot with Naan or Laccha paratha.
